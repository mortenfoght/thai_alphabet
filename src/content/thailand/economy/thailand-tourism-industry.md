# Thailand's Tourism Industry: A Pillar of the Economy

*An in-depth look at Thailand's tourism industry, covering 2025's arrival decline, shifting source markets, revenue trends, and the 2026 recovery outlook.*

Thailand's tourism industry has long functioned as one of the most visible pillars of the national economy, generating around 20% of GDP when direct and indirect effects are combined ([Vietnam News](https://vietnamnews.vn/world/1722763/thailand-struggles-to-revive-tourism-amid-sharp-drop-in-chinese-visitors.html); [Travel and Tour World](https://www.travelandtourworld.com/news/article/thailand-tourism-outlook-2026-navigating-recovery-after-a-turbulent-year-of-shocks/)). Yet 2025 delivered a jolt to that model: for the first time in a decade outside the pandemic years, international arrivals fell year-on-year, dropping 7.23% to 32.97 million visitors ([Straits Times](https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/thailand-sees-annual-foreign-tourist-arrivals-slow-to-33-million)). The disruption reflects a structural shift in Thailand's traditional source markets and underscores just how tightly tourism's fortunes are bound to the broader economy.

## 2025: A Year of Decline

Thailand welcomed 32,974,321 foreign tourists in 2025, generating 1.53 trillion baht in international tourism revenue, down 4.71% from the prior year ([Nation Thailand](https://www.nationthailand.com/news/general/40060860)). Combined with domestic tourism, total tourism revenue for the year reached 2.70 trillion baht (roughly US$86.5 billion), a 1.26% decline from 2024 ([Nation Thailand](https://www.nationthailand.com/news/general/40060629)). Domestic travel provided a partial offset, with Thai residents taking 202.37 million trips, up 2.70% year-on-year and generating 1.17 trillion baht in spending ([Nation Thailand](https://www.nationthailand.com/news/general/40060629)).

The decline followed a difficult stretch: Thailand had originally targeted at least 38 million international visitors for 2025, but severe flooding across the northern, northeastern, and southern regions in late 2025 further dented arrivals on top of the ongoing structural shift away from Chinese tourism ([BERNAMA](https://www.bernama.com/en/world/news.php?id=2509419)).

## The Chinese Tourism Collapse

No single factor explains 2025's downturn better than the collapse in Chinese visitor numbers. China was historically Thailand's largest source market, contributing nearly 28% of all arrivals with about 11.1 million visitors in 2019 ([Vietnam News](https://vietnamnews.vn/world/1722763/thailand-struggles-to-revive-tourism-amid-sharp-drop-in-chinese-visitors.html)). By 2025, Chinese arrivals had fallen to roughly 4.47 million, a drop of about 34% from the 6.73 million recorded in 2024, driven by safety concerns, stronger regional competition, and the appreciation of the Thai baht ([Visit Thailand Today](https://www.visitthailandtoday.com/thailand-tourism-statistics); [World Bank](https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/099021126000575478/txt/P513268-05951948-1534-41bc-87c7-59ff89523a00.txt)). Chinese tourist arrivals declined a sharp 37.8% year-on-year in the third quarter of 2025 alone ([World Bank](https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/099021126000575478/txt/P513268-05951948-1534-41bc-87c7-59ff89523a00.txt)). The shift was significant enough that Malaysia edged past China to become Thailand's top source market in 2025, with 4.52 million visitors versus China's 4.47 million ([Nation Thailand](https://www.nationthailand.com/news/general/40060860)).

## Long-Haul Markets Provide a Silver Lining

While short-haul Asian markets struggled, long-haul tourism set records. Thailand welcomed over 10.8 million long-haul visitors in 2025 — the first time this threshold was crossed — generating 685 billion baht in revenue, with the United Kingdom and Germany both surpassing one million visitors for the first time ([Thai Examiner](https://www.thaiexaminer.com/thai-news-foreigners/2026/06/19/long-haul-arrivals-down-1-6-but-tourism-chiefs-hope-to-recover-and-match-2025-visitor-and-income-levels/); [Travel and Tour World](https://www.travelandtourworld.com/news/article/thailand-tourism-outlook-2026-navigating-recovery-after-a-turbulent-year-of-shocks/)). India also set a new record with 2,487,319 arrivals, while Russia (1,898,837), South Korea, and other markets provided further diversification away from over-reliance on China ([Nation Thailand](https://www.nationthailand.com/news/general/40060860)).

## Revenue Resilience Despite Fewer Arrivals

A notable pattern emerged in early 2026: even as arrival numbers softened further, spending per visitor held up or even improved. In January 2026, Thailand welcomed 3.27 million foreign visitors — an 11.6% year-on-year decline — yet generated 168.6 billion baht in receipts, implying average spending of roughly 51,600 baht per visitor ([Bangkok Bank](https://www.bangkokbank.com/th-TH/-/media/b15307ea53a248ccabd88ac9591c5c34.ashx)). Long-haul visitors in particular spent an average of around 60,000 baht per trip in 2025, prompting the Tourism Authority of Thailand (TAT) to explicitly pivot its strategy toward "quality over quantity," targeting travelers who stay 14 to 21 days and spend between 65,000 and 80,000 baht per trip ([Bangkok Bank](https://www.bangkokbank.com/th-TH/-/media/b15307ea53a248ccabd88ac9591c5c34.ashx); [Nation Thailand](https://www.nationthailand.com/news/tourism/40060726)).

## 2026 Targets and Ongoing Headwinds

TAT initially set an ambitious target of 36.7 million foreign arrivals for 2026, aiming for total tourism revenue of 2.78 trillion baht, a 7% increase over 2025 ([Thailand.go.th](https://thailand.go.th/issue-focus-detail/tat-eyes-367m-foreigners-and-278tn-baht-revenue-in-2026)). That target has since been repeatedly revised downward. By June 2026, TAT had cut its forecast to just 33 million arrivals, citing high oil prices linked to Middle East unrest, which triggered a 24.9% drop in Middle Eastern tourist arrivals and weighed on global travel more broadly ([Nation Thailand](https://www.nationthailand.com/business/economy/40067283)). Through the first half of 2026, Thailand had welcomed over 16.21 million foreign tourists, down 3.11% year-on-year, generating 782.57 billion baht in revenue, with China reclaiming its position as the top source market at 2.65 million arrivals ([VietnamPlus](https://en.vietnamplus.vn/thailand-welcomes-over-1621-million-foreign-visitors-in-first-half-of-2026-post347939.vnp)).

Despite the arrival slump, Bangkok retained its title as the world's most-visited city in 2025, drawing more than 30.3 million international visitors — a testament to Thailand's enduring fundamentals as a global travel destination even amid a challenging cyclical downturn ([Thailand.go.th](https://thailand.go.th/issue-focus-detail/tat-eyes-367m-foreigners-and-278tn-baht-revenue-in-2026)).

## A New Tourist Fee and Structural Reforms

Thailand has also moved forward with a long-planned tourist entry fee, set at 300 baht for air arrivals and 150 baht for land or sea entries, now expected to take effect in mid-2026 after earlier delays. Proceeds are earmarked for improving tourist sites and funding insurance coverage for foreign visitors ([Vietnam News](https://vietnamnews.vn/world/1722763/thailand-struggles-to-revive-tourism-amid-sharp-drop-in-chinese-visitors.html)).

## Why Tourism Matters for the Wider Economy

The World Bank has flagged Thailand's slower tourism recovery as a direct drag on GDP growth, noting that weaker tourist receipts have contributed to a narrowing current account surplus, which fell to just 2.2% of GDP in the third quarter of 2025 from 8% in the first quarter ([World Bank](https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/099021126000575478/txt/P513268-05951948-1534-41bc-87c7-59ff89523a00.txt)). Because tourism touches hospitality, retail, transport, and food service sectors so broadly, its performance ripples through employment and consumption well beyond the immediate travel industry. With growth expected to remain only gradual through 2026 and 2027, tourism's ability to diversify away from dependence on any single source market — while shifting toward higher-spending, longer-staying visitors — will likely determine whether the sector can reclaim its role as one of the most reliable engines of Thai economic growth.
